## Active Macro Theses

### Thesis 1: AI Infrastructure Buildout (NOW - 2027)
**Core Belief**: AI training and inference require massive infrastructure. Companies providing power, compute, and connectivity benefit regardless of which AI lab wins.

**Value Chain**:
1. Power/Real Estate (IREN, CIFR) - Selling capacity to hyperscalers
2. Chips (NVIDIA, AMD, custom ASICs) - Compute layer
3. Interconnects/Photonics (COHR, LITE, POET) - Data movement layer
4. Networking (Arista, Ciena) - Infrastructure layer

**Key Concern (Burry, Jan 2026)**: Infrastructure-to-application ratio is 4:1 ($400B chips vs $100B AI revenue). Spending may be disconnected from returns. "Mid-cycle" - stocks may not reward further buildout.

**Monitoring**: Hyperscaler capex guidance (earnings calls), AI application revenue growth

### Thesis 2: Photonics/Optical Interconnects (2025-2028)
**Core Belief**: Electrical interconnects (copper) are hitting physical limits. 1.6T transition in 2026-2027 requires optical solutions. This is physics, not hype.

**Why It's Durable**: Even if NVIDIA GPUs get displaced by ASICs (Burry's prediction), ASICs still need optical interconnects. Photonics is beneath the GPU vs ASIC battle.

**Key Catalyst**: 1.6T transition (2026-2027)

### Thesis 3: Defense Drones & Counter-UAS (2025-2030)
**Core Belief**: Ukraine changed warfare doctrine. Pentagon shifting from expensive platforms (F-35) to attritable mass (cheap drones). NDAA bans create forced replacement of Chinese drones.

**Separate from AI thesis** - driven by geopolitics, not AI spending.

## Watchlist: AI Infrastructure / HPC

### IREN (Iris Energy) - PRIORITY

**Category**: Fast Grower (Bitcoin miner pivoted to AI/HPC)

**The Business**: Former Bitcoin miner with cheap power contracts and data center infrastructure. Pivoted to leasing GPU compute capacity to hyperscalers.

**Key Contract**: $9.7B, 5-year Microsoft deal (signed late 2025)
- Deploying 76,000 NVIDIA GB300 GPUs
- Target: $3.4B ARR by end of 2026
- 85% EBITDA margins on AI contracts

**Financials (Q1 FY26 - Sept 2025)**:
- Revenue: $240M (+355% YoY)
- Net Income: $384M (was -$51M loss)
- Cash: $1B

**Bull Case**:
- Contracted revenue provides visibility
- Microsoft deal validates execution capability
- Power assets are scarce; hard to replicate
- Trading at ~4.7x forward EBITDA if targets hit

**Bear Case**:
- Technology risk: What if NVIDIA GPUs become obsolete? Does Microsoft still need IREN's capacity?
- Execution risk: Scaling from 23K to 140K GPUs is hard
- Post-contract risk: What happens after Microsoft deal ends?
- Still has Bitcoin exposure (~10% of business)

**Burry Concern**: Stranded assets, chip obsolescence, "mid-cycle" timing

**Position Sizing**: 2-3% (Medium conviction, contracted but execution-dependent)

**Entry Criteria**:
- Already actionable at current levels
- Better entry on 20%+ pullback

**Exit Criteria (SELL IF)**:
- [ ] Microsoft contract shows signs of trouble (delays, renegotiation)
- [ ] Hyperscaler capex guidance gets cut >20%
- [ ] Miss GPU deployment milestones by >6 months
- [ ] Management turnover (CEO/CFO departure)
- [ ] Bitcoin exposure increases significantly

**Quarterly Monitoring**:
- [ ] GPU deployment progress (target: 140K by end 2026)
- [ ] Revenue vs $3.4B ARR target
- [ ] Microsoft relationship health
- [ ] New contract announcements
- [ ] Bitcoin mining as % of revenue (should be declining)

**Entry Criteria**: Actionable now; better on 15%+ pullback

**Exit Criteria**:

- [ ] 1.6T transition stalls or delays significantly
- [ ] Major customer loss
- [ ] Competitor leapfrogs on technology

**Quarterly Monitoring**:
- [ ] 1.6T transceiver revenue ramp
- [ ] Gross margin trend
- [ ] Design win announcements
- [ ] Hyperscaler order patterns

**The Business**: Leading supplier of EML lasers for 800G/1.6T transceivers. "Selling gunpowder to everyone" - even if a competitor wins a transceiver contract, they often buy lasers from Lumentum.

**Concern**: Up 372% in 2025. Valuation stretched at 9.18x sales.

**Position Sizing**: 1-2% (Wait for pullback)

**Entry Criteria**:
- Wait for 25-30% pullback from highs
- Or evidence that growth is re-accelerating

**Quarterly Monitoring**:
- [ ] Revenue growth rate (currently 58%, expected to decelerate to 32%, then 15%)
- [ ] EML market share
- [ ] Gross margin stability
